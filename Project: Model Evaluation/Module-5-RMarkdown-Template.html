Q4

Your contact at AdviseInvest has reported that utilization of sale representatives is currently at about 50% due to a poor answer rate. Could you use your model to improve utilization? Explain your answer.

Answer This particular model is predicting whether someone will purchase. Since purchasing is contingent on answering in this scenario, we have already filtered out those who didnt answer. Since this model does not include those who answered but did not purchase, we would be missing a whole segment of customers who would simply increase the time our sales reps are talking with someone. I would advise a different model be created that incorporates answering in addition to purchasing.

d %>%
  mutate(probability = predict(tree_model, type="prob")[,2]) %>%
  arrange(desc(probability)) %>%
  mutate(profit = ifelse(purchased == "yes", revenue - cost, -cost),

         customers = 1:n() / n()) %>%
  ggplot(aes(customers, cum_profit)) +

Q7 Answer

What were delivering Were providing two models to help more evenly distribute web form lead calls amoung sales reps, prioritizing those calls with higher buying potential first. We assume calls with higher purchasing potential will also be longer in duration, allowing your reps to spend more quality time with whom it will have the greatest impact. We anticipate for each hour, each rep will talk with at least one high potential customer.

Using the Models Follow the tree diagrams for both models for each lead of every hour call block. This will provide two numbers for each customer: the probability of answering and the probability of purchasing. Multiply these two numbers together for that lead. Simply sort the list of leads, descending by this final number. The resulting list will be the order in which reps should pull their next call.

Further Detail If time within each hour block is a concern, we suggest prioritizing those leads with a minimum of 50% probability of purchasing. You can see below that this will not necessarily optimize for those who answer, but there is a lot stronger likelyhood of purchasing for those who do answer. Additionally, leads with 3 checking accounts should be the highest priority. This segment has one of the highest probabilities of purchasing.

Further Improvement Assuming purchasing calls will be the longest in duration, this simple approach should improve utilization of sales reps. Additional data could further increase the time with which each rep is actively talking to a customer: the call length and the sales rep on each product sale. We anticipate a lead distribution process which includes this data would further optimize utilization by gaining a better estimate of call time and potentially increase purchases by pairing the right rep with the right lead.
